# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who need a quick, copy-ready excuse to decline a request — usually in a casual, work, or social messaging context. They may feel awkward saying no, want to soften the refusal, or just want something funny to send. The visit is short, low-stakes, and often on a phone or laptop between tasks.

## Product Purpose

Generate a polite, work-appropriate, or absurd excuse on demand, filtered by category, localized for the user's language, and copyable in one tap. Success means the user leaves with a usable sentence in their clipboard and a lighter mood.

## Brand Personality

Playful, absurd, friendly.

The voice is in on the joke without being mean. The interface should feel like a helpful little terminal that happens to dispense rejections — deadpan delivery, no unnecessary polish, and a soft undercurrent of empathy.

## Anti-references

- Corporate / earnest self-help: overly sincere language, therapy-speak framing, or motivational visuals kill the absurdity.
- Generic SaaS cream/blue landing page: over-polished startup aesthetics that fight the utilitarian, monospaced character.
- Over-animated / confetti UI: celebratory motion competes with the deadpan joke and the quick copy-paste workflow.
- Hero-metric templates: big-number social-proof blocks have nothing to do with generating excuses.

## Design Principles

1. **The words are the star.** UI chrome stays minimal so the generated excuse gets full attention.
2. **Deadpan is funnier than decoration.** Let the monospaced, terminal-like presentation deliver the humor.
3. **One tap to copy.** The fastest path from landing to clipboard is the most important path.
4. **Friendly, not cruel.** Absurd excuses are playful; the interface never mocks the user for needing one.
5. **Respect the context.** Small screens, quick sessions, and reduced-motion preferences are first-class constraints.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Broad reach / best effort. Aim for WCAG AA as a baseline: sufficient contrast for all text, visible focus indicators, full keyboard access, clear screen-reader labels on controls, and meaningful order independent of color. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` by keeping motion purposeful and providing instant fallbacks.
